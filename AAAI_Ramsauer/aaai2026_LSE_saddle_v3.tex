\begin{abstract}
\noindent
[Placeholder.]  The log-sum-exp Dense Associative
Memory~\citep{ramsauer2021hopfield} stores $M$ unit vectors $\bxi^\mu$ on
the $N$-sphere.  Ramsauer et al.\ proved $\alpha_c = 1/2$ at $T = 0$ by
modelling the inter-pattern overlaps as Gaussian.  Replacing the Gaussian
by the exact spherical density opens an interior saddle in the partition
sum at the golden ratio $\varphi^{*} = (\sqrt 5 - 1)/2$ with value
$\gmax = \tfrac12 \ln \varphi^{*} + \varphi^{*} \approx 0.3774$.  The
boundary becomes $\alpha_c(T) = 1 - \gmax - \fret(T)$, so
$\alpha_c(0) \approx 0.6226$, not $0.5$ — a factor $1.25$ in capacity.
We confirm the bound with three Monte Carlo variants (direct, semismart,
hybrid) at $N$ up to $100$.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

A Dense Associative Memory~\citep{krotov2016dense} stores $M$ patterns
$\bxi^\mu \in \mathbb{R}^N$, $\mu = 1, \dots, M$, as minima of an energy
function $H(\bx)$.  Retrieval of $\bxi^1$ from a noisy cue is the
Metropolis dynamics of $\bx$ under $H$ at external temperature $T$.  Both
the patterns and the chain live on the $N$-sphere of radius $\sqrt N$.

The log-sum-exp (LSE) variant of Ramsauer et al.\ uses
\begin{equation}
H(\bx) = -\Bnet^{-1} \ln \sum_{\mu=1}^{M} \exp(\Bnet\,\bx \cdot \bxi^\mu),
\qquad \Bnet = 1
\;\text{throughout},
\label{eq:lse}
\end{equation}
and stores $M = \e^{\alpha N}$ patterns at load
$\alpha = \ln M / N$.  The question is: what is the largest $\alpha$ at
which the chain initialised at $\bxi^1$ stays close to $\bxi^1$?

Ramsauer et al.\ answered $\alpha_c = 1/2$ at $T = 0$.  The proof
modelled the inter-pattern overlap
$\varphi_{1\mu} = \bxi^1 \cdot \bxi^\mu / N$ as a Gaussian with variance
$1/N$.  The Gaussian is the leading term of the exact spherical density
\begin{equation}
f(\varphi) \;\propto\; (1 - \varphi^2)^{(N-3)/2},
\qquad \varphi \in [-1, 1],
\label{eq:exact-density}
\end{equation}
near $\varphi = 0$, but the two densities differ near $\varphi = 1$.
That tail matters: it sets the typical maximum of $M$ samples and the
saddle of the partition sum.

This paper does three things.  First, we re-derive the boundary using
the exact density~\eqref{eq:exact-density}.  An interior saddle of the
integrand appears at the golden ratio
$\varphi^{*} = (\sqrt 5 - 1)/2$, with value
$\gmax = \tfrac12 \ln \varphi^{*} + \varphi^{*} \approx 0.3774$, and the
boundary shifts to $\alpha_c(0) = 1 - \gmax \approx 0.6226$.  Second, we
describe three Monte Carlo schemes (direct, semismart, hybrid) and
explain why a naive ``smart'' variant that refreshes its pattern cache
fails near $\alpha_c$.  Third, we confirm the corrected boundary
numerically at $N = 100$ by an escape-time measurement and by a $\phi$
residual heatmap.

\section{Theory}
\label{sec:theory}

\paragraph{Free energy split.}
For a chain in the basin of $\bxi^1$, the LSE sum is dominated by the
$\mu = 1$ term, $H(\bx)/N \approx -\varphi_1$ with
$\varphi_1 = \bxi^1 \cdot \bx / N$.  Define the energy per spin relative
to the retrieval baseline, $u(\varphi_1) = 1 - \varphi_1$, and the
$(N\!-\!1)$-sphere entropy per spin $s(\varphi_1) = \tfrac12 \ln(1 -
\varphi_1^2)$.  The retrieval free energy is the minimum of
$f(\varphi_1, T) = u - T s$:
\begin{equation}
\fret(T) = (1 - \phieq) - \tfrac{T}{2} \ln(1 - \phieq^2),
\qquad
\phieq(T) = \tfrac12 (-T + \sqrt{T^2 + 4}).
\label{eq:fret}
\end{equation}

For a chain uncorrelated with $\bxi^1$ ($\varphi_1 \approx 0$) the sum
in~\eqref{eq:lse} is dominated by the $M-1$ spurious terms
$S = \sum_{\mu \neq 1} \exp(N \varphi_{1\mu})$.  Replacing the sum by an
integral with density~\eqref{eq:exact-density} and using
$M = \e^{\alpha N}$:
\begin{equation}
S \;\approx\; \e^{\alpha N} \int_{-1}^{1}
   \exp\!\Bigl[N\,\bigl(\tfrac12 \ln(1 - \varphi^2) + \varphi\bigr)\Bigr] \dd\varphi
\;\equiv\; \e^{\alpha N} \int \e^{N g(\varphi)} \dd\varphi.
\label{eq:Sint}
\end{equation}
$g(\varphi) = \tfrac12 \ln(1 - \varphi^2) + \varphi$ has an interior
saddle at $g'(\varphi^{*}) = 0$, i.e.\
$\varphi^{*\,2} + \varphi^{*} - 1 = 0$, giving the golden ratio
$\varphi^{*} = (\sqrt 5 - 1)/2 \approx 0.618$.  Using the identity
$1 - \varphi^{*\,2} = \varphi^{*}$ the saddle value is
\begin{equation}
\gmax \;=\; g(\varphi^{*}) \;=\; \tfrac12 \ln \varphi^{*} + \varphi^{*}
        \;\approx\; 0.3774.
\label{eq:gmax}
\end{equation}
Hence $N^{-1} \ln S \to \alpha + \gmax$ and the noise-floor energy per
spin is $u_{\mathrm{noise}}(\alpha) = 1 - (\alpha + \gmax)$.

\paragraph{Boundary.}
Retrieval and noise floor cross at the boundary $\alpha_c(T)$:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;
\alpha_c(T) \;=\; 1 - \gmax - \fret(T),
\qquad
\alpha_c(0) \;=\; 1 - \gmax \;\approx\; 0.6226.
\;}
\label{eq:acsd}
\end{equation}
Compare with the Ramsauer Gaussian prediction
$\acGauss(0) = 1/2$.  The two values differ by $\gmax - (1 - 1/\sqrt 2)
\approx 0.12$ per spin — a factor $1.25$ in capacity.

\paragraph{Cusp picture.}
At fixed $\varphi_1$ the spurious sum evaluates to
$\e^{N(\alpha + \gmax)}$ to leading order, independent of $\varphi_1$.
The leading-order free energy per spin reads
\begin{equation}
\frac{F(\varphi_1)}{N} \;=\; -\max\!\bigl(\varphi_1,\,\alpha + \gmax\bigr)
   \;-\; \tfrac{T}{2} \ln(1 - \varphi_1^2),
\label{eq:F-LO}
\end{equation}
with a kink (``cusp'') at $\varphi_1 = \alpha + \gmax$.  As $T$ rises the
basin minimum $\phieq(T)$ slides toward the cusp; at
$\phieq(T) = \alpha + \gmax$ the local minimum disappears, defining the
single-pattern spinodal
\begin{equation}
\acSp(T) \;=\; \phieq(T) - \gmax,
\qquad
T_{\mathrm{sp}}(\alpha) \;=\; \frac{1 - (\alpha + \gmax)^2}{\alpha + \gmax}.
\label{eq:acsp}
\end{equation}
Both $\acSd$ and $\acSp$ end at the same point $(1 - \gmax, 0)
\approx (0.6226, 0)$.  \Cref{fig:cusp} shows the free energy at
$\alpha = 0.50$ for several $T$.

\section{Monte Carlo schemes}
\label{sec:mc}

All chains run on the $\sqrt N$-sphere at $\Bnet = 1$ with Metropolis
updates.  The variants differ in how they handle the $M = \e^{\alpha N}$
patterns.

\paragraph{Direct.}
Store all $M$ patterns explicitly and evaluate~\eqref{eq:lse} over all
of them at every step.  Memory and compute cost $\sim M N$.  Feasible up
to $N \approx 25$ at $\alpha \approx 0.7$ ($M \approx 4 \times 10^7$);
impossible at $N = 100$, $\alpha = 0.6$ ($M \approx 10^{26}$).

\paragraph{Semismart.}
Keep only the patterns with overlap $\varphi_{1\mu} > \varphi_{\mathrm{keep}}$
to the target $\bxi^1$.  The cutoff is chosen so that the expected count
above it equals a budget $K \sim 10^4$; the actual count is Poisson with
parameter $M \cdot \Pr(\varphi > \varphi_{\mathrm{keep}})$, and the $K$
overlap values are drawn from a Beta tail.  The discarded patterns enter
the LSE log-sum as the analytic constant
$\ln C_{\mathrm{bulk}} = \alpha N + \ln \int_{-1}^{\varphi_{\mathrm{keep}}}
f(\varphi)\,\e^{N \varphi}\,\dd\varphi$.  The retained set is quenched —
fixed once per disorder.  We use semismart for the wide heatmap.

\paragraph{Hybrid.}
A 1D Metropolis on the scalar $\varphi_1$.  Patterns with overlap
$c_\mu > \varphi_c = \alpha + \gmax$ are sampled from the spherical
Beta tail and kept fixed per disorder (count $K_{\mathrm{cusp}}$, of order
$M \cdot \Pr(\varphi > \varphi_c)$, in our $N = 100$ runs between $\sim
4500$ at $\alpha = 0.50$ and $\sim 8500$ at $\alpha = 0.62$).  The
contribution of the bulk ($c_\mu < \varphi_c$) is replaced by the
$\varphi_1$-independent constant $M\,\e^{N \gmax}$ from~\eqref{eq:gmax}.
Hybrid is the right tool near $\alpha_c$ at $N = 100$, where direct is
infeasible and the basin geometry is set by both the bulk floor and a
small number of cusp-tail patterns.

\paragraph{Why naive ``smart'' fails.}
A faster variant — ``smart'' — refreshes its $K$ kept patterns every
$N_{\mathrm{REFRESH}}$ steps from a cone around the current chain.  This
breaks the quenched-disorder assumption.  As the chain wanders, fresh
patterns are minted at every refresh; the total set of distinct patterns
seen along one trajectory exceeds $M$, and the chain effectively faces a
larger pattern set than the model defines.  Near the retrieval basin the
extra competitors destroy the basin.  Hybrid avoids the problem by
fixing the cusp-tail set once per disorder and never refreshing it.

\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

We measure two observables at $N = 100$:
the mean first-passage escape time $\tau$ from the retrieval basin, and
the steady-state $\avg{\varphi_1} - \phieq(T)$ residual.

\paragraph{Escape time.}
\Cref{fig:tau} reports $\log_{10} \avg{\tau}$ vs $T$ at $N = 100$ for
five $\alpha$ values, averaged over $32$ disorders, with a budget cap at
$10^5$ MC steps.  Each curve is a basin escape profile: at low $T$ the
basin is stable for the full budget (upward triangles at the ceiling);
above a critical $T_{\mathrm{esc}}(\alpha)$ the escape time drops by
several orders of magnitude over a $T$ window of width $\sim 0.01$.

$T_{\mathrm{esc}}$ moves to lower $T$ as $\alpha$ rises.  At $\alpha =
0.50$ the basin survives to $T \approx 0.085$; at $\alpha = 0.62$ it
survives only to $T \approx 0.005$.  Extrapolating to $\tau \to \infty$
at $T = 0$ gives a critical load at the right edge of the plot, near
$\alpha = 1 - \gmax \approx 0.6226$.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

The Ramsauer threshold $\alpha_c = 1/2$ is a Gaussian artefact.  The
Gaussian density misses the integrand saddle at the golden ratio that
the exact spherical density places at $\varphi^{*} = (\sqrt 5 - 1)/2$.
Including it gives $\alpha_c = 1 - \gmax \approx 0.6226$, a factor
$1.25$ larger.  The shift is per spin, not asymptotic: it appears at
leading order in $N$ and is visible already at $N = 100$.

Open items.
(i) Hot init.  All present runs start the chain at $\bxi^1$ (cold).  The
basin boundary they trace is the spinodal, not the thermodynamic
crossover; hot init at the same grid would delimit the metastability
band.
(ii) Cusp rounding at finite $N$.  The leading-order $\max(\cdot,\cdot)$
in~\eqref{eq:F-LO} is rounded on a scale $1/N$ in $\varphi_1$.  Near the
endpoint $(0.6226, 0)$ the basin offset $\phieq(T) - (\alpha + \gmax)$
falls inside the rounding window, and the leading-order spinodal slope
needs an NLO correction.
(iii) Heatmap consolidation.  \Cref{fig:heatmap} stitches three data
sources.  A clean version would use a single MC variant — semismart with
a hybrid insert near the cusp — across the entire $(\alpha, T)$ plane.
