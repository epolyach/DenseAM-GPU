¥section{Per-trial overlap distributions near the retrieval boundary}

¥subsection{Setup and observables}

We study the LSR (Log-Sum-ReLU) energy on the $N$-sphere $|¥bx|^2 = N$:
¥begin{equation}
E(¥bx) = -¥frac{N}{b}¥log¥left(¥sum_{¥mu=1}^{P}¥max¥!¥Big(0,¥;1 - b + b¥,¥frac{¥bxi^¥mu ¥cdot ¥bx}{N}¥Big)¥right),
¥label{eq:lsr}
¥end{equation}
with $b = 2+¥sqrt{2}$.  The hard wall is at $¥varphi_c = (b-1)/b ¥approx 0.707$: pattern‾$¥mu$ is ``in support'' of state‾$¥bx$ when $¥varphi_¥mu ¥equiv ¥bxi^¥mu¥!¥cdot¥!¥bx/N > ¥varphi_c$.

For each grid point $(¥alpha, T)$, the v8m simulation generates $n_{¥mathrm{dis}}$ independent disorder samples (random pattern sets $¥{¥bxi^¥mu¥}$).  Each sample produces two replicas (A, B) initialized near the target $¥bxi^1$ with $¥varphi_{¥mathrm{init}}¥in[0.75,¥,1.0]$, equilibrated for $2^{14}$ Metropolis steps, then sampled for $2^{12}$ steps.  The time-averaged overlap $¥varphi_A$, $¥varphi_B$ and the maximum spurious overlap $¥varphi_{¥max}^{¥mathrm{other}}$ are recorded per sample.

¥section{The centroid escape mechanism}

¥subsection{Support overlap between patterns}

¥paragraph{Distribution of $¥varphi_{1¥mu}$.}

¥subsection{Inter-pattern overlap and escape-enabling patterns}

¥paragraph{Relation to the thermodynamic noise floor.}
In the thermodynamic limit ($N¥to¥infty$), the maximum spurious alignment at any state on the sphere is $¥varphi_{¥max} = ¥sqrt{2¥alpha}$.  For the LSR kernel, a pattern contributes to the noise floor only if its alignment exceeds the support boundary $¥varphi_c = 1 - 1/b$.  No noise floor forms when $¥varphi_{1,¥max} < ¥varphi_c$, which from the exact extreme-value formula‾¥eqref{eq:phimax_exact} requires $¥alpha < ¥alpha_{¥mathrm{th}} = ¥tfrac{1}{2}¥ln 2 ¥approx 0.347$ (Eq.‾¥eqref{eq:alpha_th_exact}).  Below this threshold the retrieval basin is completely free of interference and retrieval is perfect at any temperature.

The centroid escape mechanism operates in a ¥emph{different} regime.  No spurious pattern is in support ¥emph{at the retrieval state} for $¥alpha < ¥alpha_{¥mathrm{th}}$, but the geometric support regions (spherical caps $¥{¥bx : ¥varphi_¥mu > ¥varphi_c¥}$) of two patterns ¥emph{do} intersect whenever their inter-pattern overlap $¥varphi_{1¥mu} > 0$---and this is always the case, since $¥varphi_{1,¥max} > 0$ for any $¥alpha > 0$.  The intersection region (the centroid) is far from the retrieval state; reaching it requires the perpendicular rotation described in Stage‾1 below.  In the thermodynamic limit the entropic cost of this rotation diverges, consistent with the vanishing noise floor.  At finite‾$N$, however, the barrier is finite.

¥paragraph{Escape-path constraint.}
For pattern‾$¥bxi^¥mu$ to participate in the centroid escape from the retrieval state at $¥varphi_{¥mathrm{eq}}$, the inter-pattern overlap $¥varphi_{1¥mu}$ must be large enough that the escape path (Stage‾1 below) fits on the sphere. ¥begin{equation}

¥label{eq:sphere_constraint}
¥end{equation}
Expanding gives the quadratic $¥varphi_{1¥mu}^2 - 2¥varphi_c¥varphi_{¥mathrm{eq}}¥,¥varphi_{1¥mu} + (¥varphi_c^2 + ¥varphi_{¥mathrm{eq}}^2 - 1) ¥leq 0$, whose smaller root defines the minimum inter-pattern overlap:
¥begin{equation}

¥subsection{Geometry of the zero-barrier escape}

We work in the two-dimensional subspace spanned by the orthonormalized pair
¥[
¥mathbf{e}_1 = ¥frac{¥bxi^1}{¥sqrt{N}}, ¥qquad
¥mathbf{e}_2 = ¥frac{¥bxi^¥mu/¥sqrt{N} - ¥varphi_{1¥mu}¥,¥mathbf{e}_1}{¥sqrt{1-¥varphi_{1¥mu}^2}}¥,.

The support conditions require $¥varphi_1 > ¥varphi_c$ and $¥varphi_¥mu > ¥varphi_c$.  These define two half-planes in the $(a,v)$ disk (Fig.‾¥ref{fig:geometry}).  The overlap region---where both patterns are simultaneously in support---is non-empty whenever $¥varphi_{1¥mu} > 2¥varphi_c^2 - 1 = 0$ (i.e., always for positive $¥varphi_{1¥mu}$).

Once both patterns are in support, the sum inside the logarithm of Eq.‾¥eqref{eq:lsr} increases, and the energy ¥emph{decreases}.  The $T = 0$ energy minimum is the geometric centroid at

¥subsection{Energetics}

The $T = 0$ energy per neuron at the retrieval state (one pattern in support) and at the geometric centroid (two patterns with $¥varphi_{1¥mu} = ¥varphi_{1,¥max} ¥approx 0.57$, equal overlap $¥varphi_{¥mathrm{cen}}^{(0)} = ¥sqrt{(1+¥varphi_{1¥mu})/2} ¥approx 0.887$) is:
¥begin{align}
¥frac{E_{¥mathrm{ret}}}{N} &= -¥frac{1}{b}¥ln¥!¥big(1 - b + b¥,¥varphi_{¥mathrm{eq}}¥big) = 0.122¥,, ¥¥
¥frac{E_{¥mathrm{cen}}^{(0)}}{N} &= -¥frac{1}{b}¥ln¥!¥big(2(1 - b + b¥,¥varphi_{¥mathrm{cen}}^{(0)})¥big) = -0.012¥,.
¥end{align}

The geometric centroid has ¥emph{lower} energy by $¥Delta E/N ¥approx -0.13$.  At finite temperature, the system thermalizes to $¥varphi_{¥mathrm{cen}}(T) ¥approx 0.83$ (Section‾¥ref{sec:two_pat_eq}), which has a higher energy than the geometric centroid but remains below the retrieval energy.  The centroid basin is thermodynamically preferred over retrieval at these finite system sizes.

¥subsection{The barrier is entropic, not energetic}

The energy barrier along the escape path is exactly zero: Stage‾1 has $¥Delta E = 0$ and Stage‾2 has $¥Delta E < 0$.  The system can reach the centroid without ever increasing its energy.

What prevents instantaneous escape is an ¥emph{entropic barrier}: the rotation in Stage‾1 must align the $(N¥!-¥!1)$-dimensional perpendicular component $¥bx_¥perp$ with a ¥emph{specific} direction ($¥bxi^¥mu_¥perp$) in a high-dimensional space.  The Metropolis random walk explores $O(1)$ new directions per step with step size $¥sigma = 2.4¥,T/¥!¥sqrt{N}$, so the typical time to discover the escape direction scales as $O(N/¥sigma^2)$.

For $N = 47$, $T = 0.325$: $N/¥sigma^2 ¥approx 3¥,400$ steps.  With $2^{14} + 2^{12} ¥approx 20¥,000$ Metropolis steps per trial, the system has $¥sim$6 independent attempts to find each escape direction.  With $n(¥varphi_{1¥mu} > ¥varphi_{¥min}) ¥sim 60$--$800$ competing patterns (Table‾¥ref{tab:n_overlap}), the total escape probability is substantial, explaining the observed centroid fractions of 7--60¥%.

The escape probability depends on ¥emph{both} the number of competing patterns (which grows with $P$ and hence $¥alpha$) and the allocated MC time, making the centroid fraction a kinetic quantity that would change with longer equilibration.

¥subsection{Two-pattern thermal equilibrium}
¥label{sec:two_pat_eq}

The geometric centroid $¥varphi_{¥mathrm{cen}}^{(0)} = ¥sqrt{(1+¥varphi_{1¥mu})/2}$ is the $T = 0$ energy minimum.  At finite temperature, the system thermalizes in the two-pattern basin, balancing the energy $u_2(¥varphi) = -(1/b)¥ln¥!¥big[2(1-b+b¥varphi)¥big]$ against the entropy $s_2(¥varphi,¥varphi_{1¥mu}) = ¥tfrac{1}{2}¥ln¥!¥big(1 - 2¥varphi^2/(1+¥varphi_{1¥mu})¥big)$ of the perpendicular subspace. ¥begin{equation}

¥subsection{Centroid hopping and the $¥varphi ¥approx 0.5$ peak}
¥label{sec:hopping}

The CDF of the per-trial overlap (Fig.‾¥ref{fig:cdf}) often shows a distinct population near $¥varphi ¥approx 0.5$, well below the two-pattern centroid at $¥approx 0.83$.  This population has a clear signature in the v8m data: $¥varphi_{¥max}^{¥mathrm{other}} ¥approx 0.82 ¥gg ¥varphi_1 ¥approx 0.48$ in every such trial.  The system is sitting at a ¥emph{spurious} two-pattern centroid $(¥bxi^¥mu, ¥bxi^¥nu)$ from which the target has been excluded.

¥paragraph{Why not a triple centroid?}
One might expect the system to recruit a third pattern into support, forming a three-pattern centroid.  The $k$-pattern symmetric centroid ($k$ patterns with common pairwise overlap‾$¥varphi_{1¥mu}$) has geometric overlap $¥varphi_k^{(0)} = ¥sqrt{¥varphi_{1¥mu} + (1-¥varphi_{1¥mu})/k}$ and as $k¥to¥infty$, $¥varphi_k^{(0)} ¥to ¥sqrt{¥varphi_{1¥mu}}$.  Whether adding patterns helps or hurts depends on a simple criterion: at the $k¥to¥infty$ centroid, is each pattern still in support?  This requires $¥sqrt{¥varphi_{1¥mu}} > ¥varphi_c$, i.e.
¥begin{equation}
¥varphi_{1¥mu} > ¥varphi_c^2 = ¥left(¥frac{b-1}{b}¥right)^{¥!2}.
¥label{eq:cluster_criterion}
¥end{equation}
For $b = 2¥!+¥!¥sqrt{2}$: $¥varphi_c^2 = 0.50$.  When $¥varphi_{1¥mu} > 0.50$, each additional pattern in the centroid ¥emph{increases} the sum inside the logarithm, lowering the energy; the $k$-pattern centroid gets deeper with‾$k$.  When $¥varphi_{1¥mu} < 0.50$, the individual ReLU arguments shrink faster than the count grows, and the two-pattern centroid is the minimum.

In the sub-threshold regime $¥alpha < ¥alpha_{¥mathrm{th}}$, the maximum inter-pattern overlap is $¥varphi_{1,¥max} = ¥sqrt{1-e^{-2¥alpha}} < ¥varphi_c$ (by definition of $¥alpha_{¥mathrm{th}}$).  Therefore $¥varphi_{1,¥max} < ¥varphi_c$, and a fortiori $¥varphi_{1,¥max} < ¥varphi_c^2$ is ¥emph{not} guaranteed---in fact $¥varphi_{1,¥max}$ may exceed $¥varphi_c^2$ while remaining below $¥varphi_c$.  Specifically, $¥varphi_{1,¥max} > ¥varphi_c^2$ when $¥alpha > -¥tfrac{1}{2}¥ln(1-¥varphi_c^4) = -¥tfrac{1}{2}¥ln(3/4) ¥approx 0.144$.

For $¥alpha < 0.144$: $¥varphi_{1,¥max} < ¥varphi_c^2 = 0.50$, the two-pattern centroid is the energy minimum.  Triple centroids are disfavored.

For $0.144 < ¥alpha < ¥alpha_{¥mathrm{th}} ¥approx 0.347$: $¥varphi_c^2 < ¥varphi_{1,¥max} < ¥varphi_c$, so multi-pattern centroids of a tight cluster would be deeper---¥emph{if} such a cluster existed.  However, forming a $k$-pattern cluster requires all $¥binom{k}{2}$ pairwise overlaps to exceed $¥varphi_c^2$.  Random patterns satisfying this are exponentially rare: the expected number of tight triples is $¥sim P^3¥,[¥Phi^c(¥varphi_c^2¥sqrt{N})]^3$, which is $¥ll 1$ at the simulation sizes.  In practice, the system hops between ¥emph{pairs}, not clusters.

¥paragraph{Centroid hopping.}
Instead of growing, the system ¥emph{hops}: from the centroid $(¥bxi^1, ¥bxi^¥mu)$, pattern‾$¥bxi^¥mu$ has its own high-overlap neighbors.  One of them, $¥bxi^¥nu$ with $¥varphi_{¥mu¥nu} > ¥varphi_{¥min}$, gets recruited via the same zero-energy perpendicular rotation---but now it is $¥bxi^1$ that is pushed out of support, not $¥bxi^¥nu$ that enters.  The result is a new two-pattern centroid $(¥bxi^¥mu, ¥bxi^¥nu)$ at comparable energy:
¥[
¥underbrace{¥bxi^1}_{¥varphi_1=0.90}
¥;¥xrightarrow{¥text{1st hop}}¥;
¥underbrace{(¥bxi^1,¥,¥bxi^¥mu)}_{¥varphi_1 ¥approx 0.83}
¥;¥xrightarrow{¥text{2nd hop}}¥;
¥underbrace{(¥bxi^¥mu,¥,¥bxi^¥nu)}_{¥varphi_1 ¥approx 0.48}
¥;¥xrightarrow{¥text{3rd hop}}¥;
¥underbrace{(¥bxi^¥nu,¥,¥bxi^¥rho)}_{¥varphi_1 ¥approx 0.1¥text{--}0.2}
¥;¥to¥; ¥cdots
¥]

After the second hop, the target is no longer in support ($¥varphi_1 < ¥varphi_c$).  Its residual overlap is set by its projection onto the active spurious pair:
¥begin{equation}
¥varphi_1 ¥;=¥; ¥frac{¥varphi_{1¥mu} + ¥varphi_{1¥nu}}{¥sqrt{2(1+¥varphi_{¥mu¥nu})}}¥,.
¥end{equation}
For $¥varphi_{1¥mu} ¥approx ¥varphi_{1,¥max} ¥approx 0.57$ and a typical $¥varphi_{1¥nu} ¥approx 0.3$--$0.4$ (the target's overlap with $¥bxi^¥nu$ is not necessarily large), this gives $¥varphi_1 ¥approx 0.40$--$0.55$, consistent with the observed peak at $¥avg{¥varphi} ¥approx 0.48$ in the v8m data.  Each subsequent hop replaces the older partner with a new neighbor of the remaining one.  The target's overlap decays with each hop as the active centroid moves further away.  Note that in LSR, the energy diverges when no pattern is in support ($¥ln 0$), so the system is ¥emph{always} at some centroid---there is no true paramagnetic state.  Trials with $¥varphi_1 ¥approx 0$ are not paramagnetic but sit at a distant centroid of patterns unrelated to the target.

¥subsection{Relation to the classical Hopfield model}

In the classical Hopfield model (Amit, Gutfreund ¥& Sompolinsky 1987), the analog of the centroid state is the ``mixture state''---a metastable configuration with equal overlap $m_n$ with an odd number‾$n$ of patterns.  However, there are fundamental differences:

¥begin{enumerate}
¥item ¥textbf{Origin.}  Classical mixture states arise from the nonlinear coupling in the Hebb rule and exist as saddle-point solutions of the mean-field equations.  LSR centroid states arise geometrically from the ¥emph{compact support} of the Epanechnikov kernel: they form wherever support regions of distinct patterns overlap on the $N$-sphere.

¥item ¥textbf{Energy.}  Classical mixture states have energy ¥emph{higher} than retrieval states (Amit et al., ¥S4, point‾4).  LSR centroids have energy ¥emph{lower} than the single-pattern retrieval minimum, because the log-sum gains from having two active ReLU terms.

¥item ¥textbf{Barrier.}  In the classical model, retrieval and mixture states are separated by barriers of order‾$N$.  In LSR, the barrier is ¥emph{zero in energy}---purely entropic.

¥item ¥textbf{Role of $P$.}  Classical mixture states exist for finite‾$p$; their number grows combinatorially but they remain metastable.  LSR centroids are the ¥emph{global minimum} for disorder realizations with nearby patterns, and their prevalence grows with $P$ through extreme-value statistics.
¥end{enumerate}

This mechanism is unique to associative memories with compact-support kernels (LSR / Epanechnikov) and has no analog in the LSE (Gaussian) model, where the exponential tail provides global support and no geometric overlap regions exist.

¥subsection{Implications for the phase diagram}

The centroid mechanism explains the three anomalies observed in the v8 phase diagram near the curved retrieval boundary at $¥alpha > ¥alpha_{¥mathrm{th}}$:

¥begin{enumerate}
¥item ¥textbf{Retrieval phase extends beyond theory.}  The theoretical boundary $¥alpha_c(T)$ assumes the ¥emph{only} minimum is the single-pattern retrieval state.  In practice, the average $¥avg{¥varphi}$ is pulled up by the centroid population (with $¥varphi ¥approx 0.83$), creating apparent retrieval beyond the boundary.

¥item ¥textbf{$q_{¥mathrm{EA}} > ¥varphi^2$.}  At the centroid, both replicas are attracted to the same two-pattern minimum: $q_{¥mathrm{EA}} = ¥bx_a ¥cdot ¥bx_b / N$ gains a contribution from the correlated perpendicular components (both aligned with $¥bxi^¥mu$), giving $q_{¥mathrm{EA}} > ¥varphi_1^2$.

¥item ¥textbf{$¥varphi - ¥varphi_{¥max}^{¥mathrm{other}} ¥approx 0.1$ at the boundary.}  At the centroid, the target and spurious overlaps are nearly equal ($¥varphi ¥approx ¥varphi_{¥max}^{¥mathrm{other}} ¥approx 0.83$), so their difference is small.  The disorder average, mixing centroid and retrieval populations, gives $¥varphi - ¥varphi_{¥max}^{¥mathrm{other}} ¥sim 0.1$.
¥end{enumerate}

¥paragraph{$¥alpha < ¥alpha_{¥mathrm{esc}}(N)$: stable retrieval.}
No pattern has inter-pattern overlap $¥varphi_{1¥mu}$ large enough for the centroid escape path to fit on the sphere. ¥paragraph{$¥alpha_{¥mathrm{esc}}(N) < ¥alpha < ¥alpha_{¥mathrm{th}}$ (including $¥alpha = 0.20$): finite-$N$ metastability.}
The thermodynamic noise floor is exactly zero: $¥varphi_{1,¥max}^{¥mathrm{exact}} = ¥sqrt{1-e^{-2¥alpha}} < ¥varphi_c$, so no spurious pattern is in support at the retrieval state.  The thermodynamic analysis correctly predicts perfect retrieval in the limit $N ¥to ¥infty$.

The intersection region---the centroid---has lower energy than the retrieval state ($¥Delta E/N ¥approx -0.073$) but occupies a vanishing fraction of the sphere ($¥varphi^T G^{-1}¥varphi = 1$ at the symmetric centroid, leaving zero perpendicular entropy per neuron).

At finite‾$N$, the entropic barrier is finite.  The perpendicular rotation (Stage‾1) requires aligning‾$¥bx_¥perp$ with a specific direction in $(N¥!-¥!1)$-dimensional space, costing $O(N)$ in entropy.  When this cost is comparable to the energy gain, the centroid becomes accessible.  The v9 data confirms that the retrieval state is metastable at $N ¥approx 47$: $¥varphi$ systematically decays with longer MC runs, even at $¥alpha = 0.22$ well below‾$¥alpha_{¥mathrm{th}}$.

The escape time should scale as $¥tau ¥sim ¥exp(c¥,N)$ with $c > 0$, making the retrieval state long-lived at moderate‾$N$ but ultimately metastable.  In the thermodynamic limit $¥tau ¥to ¥infty$ and the metastability becomes true stability.  The ``retrieval phase'' observed in the v8 Monte Carlo phase diagram is therefore a kinetic phenomenon: the system has not equilibrated past the entropic barrier within the allocated MC time.

¥paragraph{$¥alpha > ¥alpha_{¥mathrm{th}} ¥approx 0.347$: thermodynamic instability.}
The maximum spurious alignment $¥varphi_{1,¥max}^{¥mathrm{exact}} = ¥sqrt{1-e^{-2¥alpha}}$ exceeds $¥varphi_c$, creating a non-zero noise floor.  Spurious patterns interfere with retrieval even at the retrieval state itself.  The phase boundary $¥alpha_c(T)$ governs this regime; above it, retrieval fails both thermodynamically and kinetically.
